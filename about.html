About me

As a Master's student in Automation and Robotics at TU Dortmund, I am driven by the challenge of creating intelligent systems that can perceive their environment and make complex decisions in real-time. My journey, which began with a foundational background in Instrumentation and Control Engineering, has been a deliberate progression toward mastering the advanced algorithms that power modern autonomous vehicles and robots. For me, the excitement lies in bridging the gap between complex theory and tangible, real-world applications.

My core interest lies at the intersection of robotic perception and control, and I have actively sought experiences to deepen my expertise in these domains. During my research internship at Forvia Hella, I dove into the cutting-edge field of multi-modal perception, implementing event-RGB sensor fusion techniques that achieved a 30% improvement in object detection under challenging low-light conditions. This hands-on experience is complemented by my academic projects, where I have built hybrid deep learning architectures like RadarNet to enhance radar-based object detection and developed complete global and local path-planning stacks for mobile robots from the ground up.

I am a firm believer in translating theoretical models into robust, functional systems. My Master's thesis, focused on "Reinforcement Learning for Docking of the Chronos Car with Trailer," epitomizes this approach. I am not just designing a Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG) agent in simulation; I am building the complete simulation-to-real transfer pipeline to ensure its successful deployment in the physical world. This project leverages a modern robotics stack, including PyTorch, ROS2, and Gazebo, allowing me to tackle challenges from algorithm design to real-world validation.

Today, I am eager to apply my skills in reinforcement learning, sensor fusion, and control systems to solve the next generation of autonomy challenges. I am seeking to join a forward-thinking team where I can contribute to developing innovative robotic solutions and continue to grow as an engineer in a dynamic and collaborative environment.

Harsh Ramniklal Kasundra — Automation & robotics Engineering Student @ TU dortmund
